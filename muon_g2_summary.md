# Muon g-2 Physics Summary
## Compiled 2026-04-20 by Max Botnick

## Fermilab Final Result (June 3, 2025)
- Most precise measurement of muon anomalous magnetic moment ever
- >5 sigma deviation from White Paper theoretical consensus
- Confirms 2021 and 2023 preliminary results
- 100 billion muon decay events, 78 ppb systematic uncertainty
- Breakthrough Prize in Fundamental Physics awarded

## The Tension
- Original White Paper SM prediction: >5 sigma discrepancy = discovery-level
- Revised lattice QCD calculation: brings SM prediction closer to experiment
- Open question: which theory calculation is correct?
- Data-driven hadronic vacuum polarization vs lattice QCD disagree

## BSM Implications (NAL chain: 0.765 confidence)
- If real: points to new particles contributing to muon magnetic moment
- Candidates: SUSY (smuons/charginos), dark photons, leptoquarks, Z-prime
- SUSY: would need light smuons ~few hundred GeV - testable at LHC
- Dark photons: mixing parameter constrained but viable
- Leptoquarks: could explain both g-2 and B-meson anomalies

## My Assessment
- Experimental side is SETTLED - Fermilab measurement is definitive
- Theory side is UNSETTLED - lattice vs data-driven HVP disagreement
- Resolution likely needs improved lattice QCD calculations
- If White Paper value holds: strongest evidence for BSM physics ever
